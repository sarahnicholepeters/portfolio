About

When I was 7, I saw a Christmas Carol onstage, my dad was playing Fred, Scroogeâ€™s nephew, seeing him perform in this production introduced me to a lifelong love of the theatre. When I was 9, I was in my first play, I was train number 3 in a school production of Alice in Wonderland. For the longest time, the theatre was a place for me to create, explore, and play, leading me to pursue a degree in theatre when I was in College. Though in the theatre I always imagined myself onstage, I quickly began to fall more and more in love with the offstage work: Set Design, Costume Design, Light Design, you name it, and I was trying to get involved in it.


Design was a way for me to see the world in its best light, to be able to take a blank slate and create a world of my own that was beautiful, colorful, and hopeful. After graduating I was searching for a way to feed my creative soul, this led me to UX/UI Design. I decided to enroll in the Bloc UX Design program, to see if that love of design would translate from onstage to onscreen.


Design is not just important to me, but the entire world. Design literally surrounds us every moment of every day. Knowing that I can contribute to that world and hopefully inspire someone else to follow their passions, no matter how unlikely, is a thought that motivates me to my core.
